Phone charms turn an everyday accessory into a tiny expression of *you*. But
there's an art to stacking them so the look feels curated, not chaotic. Here's
how we do it.

## Start with a hero charm

Pick one **statement piece** — a character mascot, a chunky bead cluster, a
mirror charm — and build around it. This anchors the whole look. You'll find
ready-to-go combos in our [Case + Grip + Charm bundles](/devices/iphone).

## Balance weight and length

A quick rule: **vary the lengths**. One long strap, one short charm, one mid
dangle reads as intentional. Three things at the same length looks accidental.

1. Long: a beaded wrist strap
2. Medium: a character charm
3. Short: a metal initial or star

## Use color theory (lightly)

You don't need a degree — just pick **two or three colors** that repeat across
your charms. Pastels with one pop of neon is a very Y2KASE move. If your case is
busy, keep charms tonal; if your case is plain, go bold.

## Keep it secure

Nothing ruins the vibe like a lost charm. Look for:

- **Lobster clasps** over thin jump rings
- A **MagSafe grip** as a sturdy anchor point
- Removing heavy charms before tossing your phone in a bag

## Match the season

Swap charms the way you swap outfits. Hearts for February, shells for summer,
stars year-round. Browse [charms and accessories](/products?tag=phone_charm) and
keep a little rotation going.
